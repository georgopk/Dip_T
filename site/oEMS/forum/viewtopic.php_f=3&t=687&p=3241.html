Using Lumped Ports

  • Quote

Post by n4es » Tue 12 Jun 2018, 22:02

I am new to openEMS, and not much of a programmer. I have successfully simulated a 3-pole interdigital cavity band pass filter and a simple stripline transmission line stack-up with pads at the ends, using Coaxial Ports. I used the pipecap filter simulation with Coaxial Ports as a template for input, output, and s11/s21 plots. I would like to use Lumped Ports to reduce the simulation volume and speed up the simulations. I cannot seem to get correct results when I try to convert to Lumped Ports. I have tried to understand the Patch Antenna Phased Array Tutorial (3 ports, 2 files) and the Patch Antenna Example (1 port). I attempted to add the computations from the 1-port Patch Antenna example, but could not figure out how to extend to 2 ports. I would like to plot s11 and s21 directly, and then hopefully to save 2-port s-parameters as an S2P file. It would be helpful to see a simple example, contained in a single .m file, that is focused on plotting and saving 2-port s-parameters, without field plots. Such a template could then be applied to various CSX geometries, which are not a problem. Any guidance or examples would be appreciated. Thanks! Bill N4ES

Post by n4es » Mon 18 Jun 2018, 18:45

I found that I can use short coaxial ports (1-inch) at 300 MHz if I use a fine mesh: mesh_res = c0/(f0+fc)/unit/500.
I am now getting good agreement between stripline z0 and coaxial port z0 when looking at a quarter-wave stripline. Seeing 1.11:1 maximum VSWR, or 1.05:1 ratio between coaxial port z0 and stripline z0. Error is probably meshing of the small coaxial center conductor. This is a 75-ohm simulation.
I still can't get results I believe with lumped ports, so I'll stick with the coaxial ports.
Thank you for openEMS!!!
