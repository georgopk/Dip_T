I had a look at your script. You cannot/should not use the FDTD method to simulate the antenna AND the entire environment (aka room?)
You have 111 millions cells with a way to coarse air resolution... This is insane!

What you can/need to do is simulate the antenna plus feeding with openEMS and take the pattern to feed it into some ray-tracing algorithm...
Everything else is insanity...
Maybe some boundary element method could handle this as you only need to discretize the boundaries, but a volume method like FDTD or FEM cannot do this and if they could it would be a waste of resources.

One other approach using only FDTD would be (but this is currently not implemented in openEMS):
1: Simulate the antenna (with openEMS or others) record the fields on a boundary surrounding the antenna (huygens box)
2: Create the room (without the antenna) and excite an equivalent field using the recorded huygens box (similar to a ray-tracing algorithm, but this is not available in openEMS)

Oh, no. Starting from ver 11 HFSS solves such structures easily. (we were using HFSS13 and ED2015). Inhomogeneous meshing makes miracles! You see, the advantage is that the solution is locked like on ports and boundaries, and distribution looks coherent even with coarse meshes (of course S-params would be wrong, but you can anylyze where to improve). I would say, very dense mesh is bad for FEM because small deltas at finite precision can amplify numerical noise, especially for 2nd order solutions. In FEM error rapidly distributes and amplifies giving insane pictures for mesh step anything bellow l/10. I've talked with a guy at IMS, who however was developing criteria and automeshing method for solving with l/4 steps (l/8) with minimal accumulated error... but I did not hear from him from that time.
The problem of HFSS is that it places ALL the matrix once in coherent memory, and the memory amount should be large, depending on the precision...But the speed is acceptable, thanks to coarse meshing in weak-amplitude areas. And of course, you can not see the signal propagation in FEM, only S-params and CW power maximums. I did not use HFSS Transient yet.


Unfortunately I do not know any ready-to-use ray-tracing tool, callable from Octave/Matlab, or just from console...
