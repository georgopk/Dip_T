Post by thorsten » Thu 06 Oct 2016, 20:05

Hi Erik,

I assume with neighboring you mean touching at a mesh surface. In this case it is a normal and expected behavior as this touching counts as overlapping

The reason is the different ways how metal (PEC) and normal (bulk) material is discretized.
Materials are discretized as cell filling materials that gets averaged into the FDTD coefficients. But for metal (AddMetal --> PEC) the electric field located at the center of the mesh edges is short-circuited (set to zero) if a metal is present there. But if a material with higher priority is present too, this will override the metal. Thus material can override metals on mesh edges or surfaces (many edges) ...
Two touching materials would not show this effect...

It is all quite complex, but I hope you could follow my explanation...

regards

This is by the way also the reason why you can have 1D or 2D metals (PEC), but not as "normal"/bulk material, even if you define it to be a metal with a finite conductivity...

BUT, you can define a 1D or 2D material to cut out 1D or 2D metals and at the same time the material does have no further influence...
Thus it doesn't even matter what material properties you give it... but this is all very advanced ...

Just keep in mind, priorities can even matter for touching objects...

Post by thorsten » Thu 06 Oct 2016, 22:36

Hi,

just use priorities properly and no further steps are necessary. Just give metals a higher priority than your (dielectric) materials, e.g. substrates, and again higher priorities to materials that are meant to cut out metal parts if necessary.

Small oversizes can always be helpful in case the mesh lines are complex fractions.
In this case for example, 1D or 2D metals may otherwise not hit the edges and thus get not recognized at all. But if they are missed completely openEMS warns about unused objects.
I actually never do this but rather take care of proper meshing, e.g. using micrometer as drawing unit and rely on integer values only...

Re: Regarding materials, box priorities, and mesh coordinate

  • Quote

Post by thorsten » Sun 23 Apr 2017, 19:23

It would not matter in this case

For "normal" materials it only matters how the cells are filled to calculate the FDTD coefficients. The mesh edges (and thus the mesh planes too) only matter for perfect metal (PEC) materials (and excitations).
Because in this case the coefficients are set to 0 (because zero E-field inside a perfect metal).
For case one this would mean the edge is either metal or not, case two would be undefined and should be avoided.
Just as a rule of thump, try to avoid properties with identical priorities. There exist enough numbers to do this right?
