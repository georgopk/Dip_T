Post by thorsten » Wed 19 Nov 2014, 21:13

Hi,

doing an auto meshing is really really difficult. We actually have trouble just creating a reliable smoothing algorithm. You would think that this should be easy, but not even this is...
Even the commercial tools have really trouble getting the auto meshing right... I would go so far to say that it is close to impossible, because doing a really good mesh is maybe the most important part which requires all the user experience in modeling.

In your case you should have a closer look at the DetectEdges function. There is a feature to detect edges and apply a 1/3-2/3 rule:
https://github.com/thliebig/CSXCAD/blob ... dges.m#L20

If you combine that with only detecting certain properties (SetProperty option) and running a smoothing afterwards you may get what you are looking for.

Please tell me what you think and have found...

regards
Thorsten

Would you direct me to the right place/post or perhaps and example?
Have a look at the CRHL tutorials. But you will have to find your own way of doing it. What I tend to do is, at the time I create an object, I define some mesh lines for that object. At the end I collect all these and just smooth them out wit a global (coarse) mesh resolution. But doing the mesh is the real "art" of EM simulation. Everything else is easy, doing a good mesh is what determines your results and simulation effort...
