Post by Hale_812 » Tue 14 Mar 2017, 06:43

welll... I've been trying to optimize chiral metamaterial lens array components... without luck. to make multidimensional optimizations you have to define your own algorithm with state of art weighting and logical conditioning. in binary arrays people use cunning genetic algorithms looking up for candidate solutions, and then in the end a human sorts out meaningless solutions.



So, good point here is that OpenEMS is well linked to Octave where you can practice with algorithms a lot.
The bad thing is that Octave is not well multithreaded, and OpenEms is not fast enough for step-by step optimizations. You need to kill awfully lot of time organizing a cluster management in Octave, and then sending dozens of "just guessed" candidate configurations to be solved simultaneously...
