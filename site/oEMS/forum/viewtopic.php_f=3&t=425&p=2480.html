Time-resolved E-field probes at range?

  • Quote

Post by EMmodeller2016 » Fri 09 Dec 2016, 11:53

Hi

OpenEMS is a fantastic piece of software and thanks for sharing it. I've made good progress since starting using it a week ago but have reached an impasse. I am trying to modelling the transient behaviour of an antenna (i.e. using custom excitations) and would particularly like to know what the time-resolved E-field components are at some arbitrary location outside the simulation/mesh box please. Obviously it's not practical to set up a mesh to include free-space propagation in the far field. Unfortunately, as far as I can tell, NF2FF seems to strip out all time-resolved information from the simulation, which prevents me from inferring E(t) at an arbitrary point beyond the mesh.

I've used a commercial FDTD EM package in the past and it supports so-called far-field probes. These can exist outside the mesh (I think) and can carry out the operation I described. Is there anything similar in openEMS please or a simple route to generate the data I want?

Thanks in advance

Hi,

there is only the nf2ff which indeed only works for given frequencies and not in time domain. But I think there exist implementations for time-domain nf2ff. Maybe you can do some search.
In theory you could implement these (at least at first) just in Matlab/Octave by reading and analyzing the recorded near-fields in time domain.
Actually the very first nf2ff was implemented as matlab code too, but to get much greater speed I reimplemented it in C/C++.
With other words, a nf2ff in time-domain does not (yet) exist, but maybe you could have a look and change that

Hi EMmodeler,

yo can do an nf2ff calculation for a sufficiently dense frequency array at a fixed radius, phi and theta. Then just do an inverse FFT. This gives you the time domain signal at that point. The frequency array must be dense enough to cover the whole duration of your excitation signal. Otherwise wou will see time domain aliasing.

Cheers
Georg
