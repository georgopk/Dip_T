Good afternoon,
I added a voltage probe to my simulation.
The probe is just a flat surface somewhere in space.
Due to varying magnetic field there is some voltage induced.

I also added E and H field dumps at the same position as the voltage probe.

Following Maxwell's equations, I integrated the E field around the edge of this surface.
I also integrated the magnetic flux through this surface and took its time derivative.
These two voltages agree very well.

But the voltage probe is pretty far off.
Why?
Shouldn't it be just the integral of all edge voltages along the integration line?
If yes, shouldn't it agree with my own integration?

Post by thorsten » Mon 22 May 2017, 18:14

Shouldn't it be just the integral of all edge voltages along the integration line?
No not at all. Usually the voltage probe should be a line, because it integrates from start to stop only.
If it is a plane it will still integrate from start to stop but this time it will be kind of the diagonal.

The current probe is a different story. It does integrate the magnetic field along its boundary to calculate the current though its plane.

One more note, be aware of the locations of the electric fields and magnetic fields in the Yee scheme and if you use interpolations for the field dumps as this matters much here...

Did that answer your question?
