Hi,

may first question usually is, why do you need this

But I guess a hard magnetic field source should look like what you want?
Of course it is a bit more difficult to set up, because you have to define a closed loop of a hard magnetic field that if integrated gives your desired current.
To do this you have to remember that the magnetic field is defined on a secondary mesh. See the FDTD wiki for more details.
Thus you would need to define your hard magnetic source in the middle of the mesh and four pieces of it.

One more note. Remember that if you excite with a Gaussian pulse, only the peek of that pulse will have your given value. In frequency domain, this value will be much smaller. But this is all a matter of scaling since Maxwell’s equations are linear.

Did this help?

I'm still waiting for an answer why you need an ideal current source, after all that does not exist in reality ...

I think what he did is create 4 hard magnetic excitations placed around one mesh edge exciting the 4 tangential magnetic fields surrounding that edge. What you need to be aware of is, that the magnetic fields are located in the center of the faces of each primary mesh cell. That way each mesh edge (where a electric field is located) is surrounded by four tangential magnetic fields...
